Dolphins are commonly found in warm, shallow waters of coastal areas but can also be found in deep oceanic environments. They often inhabit areas near the continental shelf, where food is abundant.

Octopuses live in a variety of ocean habitats, from shallow reefs to the deep sea, often hiding in crevices or burrows. They prefer rocky or sandy ocean floors where they can easily find shelter and prey.

Sea turtles inhabit both warm and temperate ocean waters, often migrating between feeding grounds and nesting beaches. They can be found in coastal habitats like coral reefs, seagrass beds, and beaches.

Clownfish live in the sheltered environments of coral reefs, where they form symbiotic relationships with sea anemones. The anemones provide protection for the clownfish, while the fish help keep the anemones clean.
