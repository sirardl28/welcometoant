Dolphins are carnivorous and primarily feed on fish, squid, and other marine animals, using their echolocation to hunt. They often work together in groups to herd and catch their prey.

Octopuses are opportunistic predators, feeding on crabs, fish, and other invertebrates. They use their strong beaks to break open shells and their arms to catch and manipulate their food.

Sea turtles have varied diets depending on their species, with some eating seagrass, while others feed on jellyfish, crustaceans, and small fish. Their feeding habits help maintain the balance of the ecosystems they inhabit.

Clownfish primarily eat small zooplankton, algae, and occasionally tiny invertebrates. They also benefit from eating the leftover food of their host anemone, creating a mutualistic feeding relationship.
